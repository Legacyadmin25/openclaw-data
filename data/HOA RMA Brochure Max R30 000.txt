Rand Mutual Assurance – Terms and Conditions
Eligibility for Coverage
1. All application forms must be fully and accurately completed for the member and their
dependents, including full names, dates of birth, and identification document numbers.
2.Dependent Children Limit A maximum of 6 children can be classified as dependents.
Children under 21 years (or 26 years if a full-time student) are eligible.
3. Coverage for Disabled Children Mentally disabled, permanently disabled, and totally
disabled children are covered until their death or as long as the principal member remains
enrolled in the scheme.
4.Spousal Coverage The scheme recognizes common-law, civil, and tribal marriages. Only
one spouse can be listed as a dependent, provided they are legally or traditionally married to
the member.
5.Accidental Death Coverage
Coverage takes effect immediately after the first premium is received.
6.Claims Submission Deadline
Claims must be submitted to Legacy Guard Brokers within 12 months of the death of an
assured. Late submissions will not be considered.
7. Claims Settlement Process
Claims will be settled upon receipt of certified documents, including:
Death certificate
Copies of the deceased’s and claimant’s IDs
Underwriter’s Insurance Certificate
Any other required documentation
Surviving Spouse as Principal Member
If the principal member dies, the surviving spouse can apply to become the new Principal
Member by notifying Legacy Guard Brokers in writing.

TERMS & CONDITIONS
8.Suicide Clause No benefit will be paid for deaths resulting from suicide or attempted
suicide within: 12 months from policy inception, or 2 years of reinstating a lapsed policy.
9.Exclusions from Coverage
Death benefits will not be paid if caused by:
9.1 Terrorism, war, civil unrest, or military conflicts.
9.2 Radioactive contamination, nuclear, biological, or chemical weapons.
9.3 Suicide (unless all policy conditions are met).
9.4 Self-injury, alcohol/drug influence, or insanity (unless medically prescribed).
9.5 Involvement in unlawful activities by the member or dependents.
9.6 Divorced spouses (coverage ceases immediately upon divorce).
10.Waiting Period for New Members
10.1 A 6-month waiting period applies to all new members from the policy start date.
10.2 Waiting Period for Extended Family
Extended family members are subject to a 6-month waiting period, regardless of age.
10.3 Definition of Extended Family
Includes parents, parents-in-law, and other blood relatives specified by the Principal
Member.
11.Coverage Limits
11.1 The scheme covers the Principal Member + 16 dependents (spouse, children, and
extended family).
11.2 Child Benefit Payout Structure - dependent children will receive a portion of the cover
amount based on age:
11.3 Age Restrictions for Extended Family
Maximum age for extended family: 84 years at joining.
Member Plus Groups may include:
Up to 4 parents/parents-in-law/grandparents (<84 years).
Up to 4 other dependents (<64 years), including uncles, aunts, siblings, and cousins.

TERMS & CONDITIONS
12. Premium Payment Responsibility
The Principal Member must ensure monthly premiums are paid on time and in full.
13.Policy Lapse Conditions
Failure to pay 2 consecutive premiums (or arrears equal to 2x premiums) will result in policy
lapse.
14. Required Claim Documents
Submit the following to claims@scarletcapital.co.za:
14.1 Completed Death Claim Notification Form.
14.2 Original death certificate (SAPS-certified).
14.3 Computerized death certificate (no abridged copies).
14.4 Certified copies of IDs (deceased, claimant, and third-party if applicable).
14.5 Beneficiary’s certified ID.
14.6 Certified BI-1663 form (SAPS).
14.7 Bank details & statement of payee/beneficiary.
14.8 Police statement (for unnatural deaths).
14.9 Medical report (stillborns after 26 weeks).
14.10 Marriage certificate (if no spouse listed).
14.11 Proof of children (if none listed).
15. Additional Documentation
RMA may request extra documents (e.g., medical records, proof of relationship) depending
on the claim.
16. Common-Law/Religious Marriage Claims
Claims for such spouses only valid if they were listed as dependents or added via amendment
form before death.
17. Benefit Payment - Valid claims will be paid to the designated beneficiary listed on the
policy.

18.Claims Deadline Reminder - Claims must be submitted within 12 months of death; late
claims are void.
19. Fraud & Misrepresentation -RMA reserves the right to cancel policies and forfeit
premiums if fraud is detected. Legal action may follow.
20. Policy Cancellation by Member
Members may cancel within 30 days of policy receipt by submitting a written request.
21. Cancellation Conditions
Cancellation only allowed if no claims were paid or events occurred. Risk cover costs may be
deducted.
22.Refund on Cancellation
Premiums will be refunded minus costs incurred during the coverage period.
