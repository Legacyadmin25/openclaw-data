TERMS & CONDITIONS
Old Mutual Funeral Plan – Terms and Conditions
1. Definitions
Policyholder: The person who owns this funeral policy.
Life Assured: The person(s) covered under this policy.
Beneficiary: The person(s) nominated to receive the funeral benefit.
Premium: The monthly amount payable by the Policyholder to keep the policy active.
Insurer: Old Mutual Life Assurance Company (South Africa) Limited.
2. Eligibility
South African residents aged 18 years and older, including persons 85 years and above,
may be insured.
Spouse, children, and extended family may be included as additional lives assured.
No medical examinations or health questions are required for cover at any age.
3. Waiting Periods
Natural death (all ages): A waiting period of 6 months applies from the policy start
date.
Accidental death: Covered immediately from policy start date.
If the policy lapses and is reinstated, waiting periods may reapply.
4. Child Benefit Limits
In accordance with the Long-term Insurance Act and FSCA rules, the following maximum
benefits apply to children:
Stillborn child (after 26 weeks of pregnancy): R10,000
Child under 6 years old: R10,000
Child 6–13 years old: R30,000
Child 14–21 years old: R50,000 (if a full-time student or financially dependent)
Children 21 years and older: Count as adults, eligible for the full benefit (up to
R100,000).
If a higher benefit is selected for a child, the payout will be reduced to the legal
maximum for that age.

5. Policy Benefits
A lump sum funeral benefit is payable to the nominated beneficiary(ies) on the death of
a life assured.
The benefit is intended for funeral expenses and is paid in cash.
Cover amounts and premiums are specified in the Policy Schedule.
Repatriation Benefit
This policy includes a repatriation service within South Africa.
In the event of death away from the normal place of residence, the body will be
transported to the selected funeral home closest to the place of burial within South
Africa.
This benefit is available 24/7, and is provided in addition to the funeral cash benefit.
6. Exclusions
Benefits will not be paid if death results from:
Terrorism or war (declared or not).
Radioactive contamination.
Death resulting from illegal activities.
Suicide within the first 12 months of cover commencement.
Divorced spouses at inception of the policy (cover ceases immediately if divorce occurs
during the term).
7. Premiums, Grace Period and Lapse
Premiums are payable monthly in advance.
Grace Period: One month is allowed for the payment of overdue premiums. Cover
remains active during this time.
If the arrear premium is not paid by the end of the second month, the plan will lapse.
Lapse: If two consecutive premiums are missed (including grace), cover ends.
8. Reinstatement
A lapsed policy may be reinstated after two months.

9. Cooling-Off Period
A 31-day cooling-off period applies from the date of receiving the policy.
If no claim has been made, the policyholder may cancel in writing, and premiums paid
will be refunded (less costs of risk cover and administration).
10. Cancellation
After the cooling-off period, either the policyholder or Old Mutual may cancel the
policy by giving one month’s written notice.
11. Policy Review
Premium rates are not guaranteed and may be reviewed annually or sooner if claims
ratios or scheme risks change.
Old Mutual will give 3 months’ written notice before any premium increase.
The policyholder may accept new rates, request a benefit reduction, or cancel the
policy.
Failure to pay the revised premium will result in benefit reduction or cancellation.
12. Dissolution
On dissolution of the plan, Old Mutual’s liabilities towards principal members and
dependents will cease from the date of the last premium received or the date of dissolution,
whichever is later.
13. Continuation Option
If a Principal Member leaves a group or scheme, he/she has 30 days to convert to an
individual funeral policy with Old Mutual.
Waiting periods under the new policy will apply for natural death.
Accidental death remains covered immediately.
14. Termination of Policy
The policy terminates when:
The policyholder cancels in writing.
Premiums remain unpaid beyond the grace/lapse period.
The last life assured has died and all benefits are paid.
