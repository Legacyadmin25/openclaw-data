1. DEFINITIONS
Policyholder: The person who owns the policy and is
responsible for premium payments.
Life Assured: Any person covered under the policy.
Beneficiary: The person nominated to receive benefits.
Premium: Monthly payment required to maintain cover.
Insurer: Rand Mutual Assurance (RMA) and Old Mutual Life
Assurance Company (SA).
Administrator: House of Administration acting as intermediary
and administrator.
2. LEGAL AND REGULATORY COMPLIANCE
This policy complies with the Long-term Insurance Act, FAIS
Act, and FSCA regulations.
All benefits are subject to statutory limits, especially for minors.
No advice is provided unless through a licensed representative.
All members must receive full disclosure documentation before
inception.
3. ELIGIBILITY AND MEMBERSHIP
South African residents aged 18 years and above may join.
Dependents include spouse, children, and extended family.
Maximum dependents and entry ages apply per scheme rules.
Children may be covered up to age 21 (or 26 if full-time
student).
4. WAITING PERIODS
Natural death: 6-month waiting period from inception.
Accidental death: Covered immediately.
Reinstated policies may have waiting periods reapplied.
No claims payable during waiting period except accidental
deaths.
5. CHILD BENEFIT STRUCTURE (SUBJECT TO FSCA LIMITS)
Old Mutual vs RMA: % Of Main Member Cover
Stillborn (6 months+): OM 10% | RMA 12.5%
0–5 years: OM 25% | RMA 25%
6–13 years: OM 50% | RMA 50%
14–21 years: OM 100% | RMA 100%
All benefits capped as per Long-term Insurance Act regardless
of selected cover.
6. POLICY BENEFITS
A lump sum benefit is payable upon death of a life assured.
Benefit amount is stated in the policy schedule.
Payment is made to nominated beneficiary.
Benefits are intended for funeral expenses.
7. REPATRIATION BENEFIT (OLD MUTUAL ONLY)
Includes transportation of the deceased within South Africa.
Available 24/7 via 0860 60 60 60.
Applies from place of death to nearest funeral home or place of
burial.
This benefit is separate from the cash payout.

8. PREMIUMS AND PAYMENT TERMS
Premiums are payable monthly in advance.
Grace period: 30 days.
Failure to pay within 60 days results in policy lapse.
No cover during unpaid period beyond grace.
Premiums may be reviewed annually with notice.
9. CLAIMS PROCESS
Claims must be submitted within 12 months of death.
Required documents include:
- Certified ID copies
- Death certificate
- DHA-1663 form
- Banking details
- Additional documents if required
Claims will only be processed once all documents are received.
10. EXCLUSIONS
No benefit payable for:
- War, terrorism, civil unrest
- Nuclear/radioactive events
- Illegal activities
- Suicide within first 12 months
- Fraud or misrepresentation
11. MISREPRESENTATION AND FRAUD
Any false information may result in policy cancellation.
Claims may be rejected if material facts were not disclosed.
Legal action may be taken in cases of fraud.
12. POLICY LAPSE AND REINSTATEMENT
Policy lapses after non-payment of 2 consecutive premiums.
Reinstatement may be allowed subject to underwriting.
Waiting periods may reapply after reinstatement.
13. COOLING-OFF PERIOD
A 31-day cooling-off period applies.
Full refund provided if no claim occurred.
Risk and admin costs may be deducted.
14. CANCELLATION
Policyholder may cancel with written notice.
Insurer may cancel with valid reason and notice.
No refunds after risk cover has been provided.
15. COMPLAINTS AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION
Complaints must first be directed to the Administrator.
If unresolved, escalate to: FAIS Ombudsman
Ombudsman for Long-term Insurance
16. CONTINUATION OPTION
Members leaving a scheme may convert to individual cover.
New policy terms and waiting periods may apply.
17. GENERAL CONDITIONS
Policy terms may change with notice.
All communication must be in writing.
This document must be read with the policy schedule.
